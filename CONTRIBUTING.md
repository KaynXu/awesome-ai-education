# Contributing

Thanks for improving `Awesome AI + Education`.

## What To Submit

- AI-powered education tools with a concrete teaching, learning, assessment, feedback, tutoring, school operations, or curriculum workflow.
- Education-focused open-source projects that a reader can inspect, run, or learn from.
- Public case studies that show how AI was implemented in a real classroom, school, district, university, or education team.
- Emerging work with enough public detail to explain why it belongs on the watchlist.

## What Not To Submit

- General AI learning courses.
- Prompt packs or AI literacy resources without an applied education workflow.
- Generic AI tools where the education use case is only hypothetical.
- Paper-only resources without practical implementation evidence.
- Broken links, private demos, or resources with no public description.

## Submission Requirements

Each submission should include:

- The target users.
- The education workflow.
- How AI is applied.
- Why the entry belongs in `AI + Education`.
- The best category for the entry.
- Any public evidence, implementation notes, or case-study context.

## Style

- Keep descriptions concise and factual.
- Avoid marketing claims that are not visible from the public source.
- Use one external link per entry.
- Mirror meaningful README changes in both English and Chinese when possible.
