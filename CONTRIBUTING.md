# Contributing

Thank you for helping build `Awesome AI for Education` into a useful field guide for education practitioners.

You may submit in English only. Maintainers are responsible for keeping the English and Chinese READMEs aligned before merge.

## Start With The Scope

This repository covers AI that improves educational practice. A submission must support a concrete workflow in teaching, learning, assessment, feedback, tutoring, curriculum, school operations, governance, or educator development.

Do not submit general AI-literacy courses, prompt collections, generic AI tools with hypothetical education uses, paper-only resources without a practical implementation, private demos, or broken links.

## Choose A Layer

- Main List: A practical resource with a clear target user, inspectable workflow, stable public URL, and public evidence.
- Open Source: An education-focused codebase with an inspectable license, setup path, workflow, and maintenance history.
- Field Evidence: A named education implementation with enough context to understand the setting, workflow, AI role, and source owner.
- Watchlist: A relevant resource that is early, experimental, weakly evidenced, or difficult to inspect.

Open source is a separate layer, not a requirement for the Main List. A maintainer may move a suggestion to a different layer.

## Suggest A Project

Use the [project suggestion form](https://github.com/KaynXu/awesome-ai-education/issues/new?template=suggest-project.yml). Include:

- Project name and primary public URL.
- Requested layer.
- Target users and concrete education workflow.
- The role of AI in that workflow.
- Best public evidence URL and evidence owner.
- Access model or open-source status.
- Your affiliation and any self-promotion or material relationship.

## Suggest Field Evidence

Use the [field evidence form](https://github.com/KaynXu/awesome-ai-education/issues/new?template=suggest-case-study.yml). Include:

- Named education setting and public source URL.
- Implementation scale and participants.
- Education workflow and role of AI.
- Observed result or operational change.
- Source owner and whether the source is vendor-published.
- Your affiliation and any material relationship.

Vendor-published cases are welcome when labeled. They are not treated as independent evaluation.

## Submit A Pull Request

Before editing, read the [curation criteria](docs/criteria.md) and [evidence ledger](docs/evidence.md). Keep each entry to one factual sentence naming the workflow, target user, and strongest evidence type. Do not add marketing tags, star counts, or numeric editorial scores.

A contributor may update only the English README. A maintainer must add or verify the corresponding Chinese entry before merge. Do not duplicate a project across workflow sections.

## Review Flow

```text
Suggestion -> scope screen -> evidence review -> layer decision -> human approval -> bilingual merge -> periodic review
```

Inclusion is an editorial decision, not an automatic reward for completing the form. Maintainers may request stronger evidence, move a resource to Watchlist, or decline it. Removing a weak or stale entry is also a valuable contribution.
