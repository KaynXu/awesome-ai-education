# Initial Curation Checklist

## Coverage Targets

- 6 to 10 populated top-level categories.
- 20 to 40 total entries before broad promotion.
- At least four implementation case studies.
- At least one open-source project.
- A small watchlist for emerging work that is promising but not fully validated.
- English and Chinese README files kept broadly aligned.

## Review Questions

- Is the education workflow explicit?
- Is the AI application clear?
- Is the target user clear?
- Is the public link stable and useful?
- Does this belong in the main list, case studies, open source, or watchlist?
- Would a reader understand why this is `AI + Education`, not just general AI?
- Does the wording avoid unsupported hype?

## SEO and GEO Checks

- The README uses natural phrases such as `AI education tools`, `AI teaching tools`, `AI tutoring`, `AI assessment`, `AI feedback`, `EdTech AI`, and `AI in education`.
- The entry description states the workflow and AI role in plain language.
- `llms.txt` and `metadata.json` stay aligned with the README scope.
- The GitHub topics include `awesome`, `awesome-list`, `ai-in-education`, `edtech`, `education-technology`, `generative-ai`, and related discovery terms.
